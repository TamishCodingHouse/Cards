Youtube

YouTube, founded by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley, and Jawed Karim, has grown to become the largest video-sharing platform in the world since its launch on February 14, 2005. It is renowned for revolutionizing the way people consume and share video content online. Users can upload, view, and share videos on a wide range of topics, from entertainment and education to vlogs and tutorials.

Twitter

Twitter, founded by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams, was officially launched on July 15, 2006. The platform is renowned for its role as a microblogging service where users post brief messages called "tweets," limited to 280 characters. It has become a hub for real-time news, trending topics, and influential conversations, with users ranging from celebrities and politicians to everyday individuals.

Instagram

Instagram, founded by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger, has become one of the most popular social media platforms since its launch on October 6, 2010. It is best known for its focus on photo and video sharing, allowing users to capture moments and share them with their followers through posts, stories, and reels. Instagram's visually driven format, coupled with its user-friendly interface and creative editing tools.
